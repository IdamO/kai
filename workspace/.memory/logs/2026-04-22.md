## 03:15 [COMPLETED] Background Research: Contrast-vs-overlap Bridge A/B design
Output: `.claude/research-results/2026-04-22-contrast-vs-overlap-bridge-ab.md` (14.1KB).
Queue: P1 item flipped DONE; 4 new follow-ups added (1 P1 bridge.py fix, 2 P2, 1 P3 paper).

Three findings that reshape the A/B scope vs the queue-item spec:
1. **N=20 is severely underpowered.** Even at 30% baseline re-share rate, 20 vs 20 needs a 40pp absolute lift (2.4x fold) to detect at 80% power. Realistic discovery-feature interventions produce 1.5-2x lifts. Need n~196/arm for 2x-lift detection at 10% baseline. Downgrade P1 from "falsification A/B" to "N=20 directional pilot" — prior-updating Bayesian exercise, not frequentist test.
2. **DeepPref cosine is the wrong contrast metric.** It's trained on preference labels => inherits overlap bias. Using it to define contrast would conflate model-disagreement with perceptual distance. Use conjunction of raw MERT L24 cosine (inverted) + multi-feature L2 distance (BPM/key/loudness/acousticness/energy), top quartile on BOTH.
